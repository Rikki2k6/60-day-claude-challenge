# Personal Financial Command Center – Money Pulse

Today I built **Money Pulse**, an interactive personal financial command center that helps students understand, manage, and improve their monthly finances. The application works entirely inside the browser without requiring any backend or external services, making it fast, private, and easy to use. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

The dashboard combines budgeting, expense tracking, savings planning, financial health analysis, and interactive simulations into a single interface.

---

## Features

### Financial Health Dashboard

The application generates an overall **Financial Health Score** based on several financial indicators, including:

- Fixed expense ratio
- Budget discipline
- Savings cushion
- Goal progress
- Subscription load

The score is visualized using a circular health meter along with personalized financial insights.

---

### Income & Expense Management

Users can manage their complete monthly finances by adding:

- Monthly income
- Fixed obligations (rent, EMI, bills)
- Variable expenses
- Subscriptions

All calculations update instantly as values change.

---

### Budget Tracking

Variable spending categories include both planned budgets and actual spending.

The dashboard highlights:

- Budget utilization
- Overspending
- Remaining budget
- Spending progress bars

This makes it easy to identify where money is being lost.

---

### Savings Goals

Users can create multiple financial goals, such as:

- Laptop purchase
- Emergency fund
- Travel savings

Each goal displays:

- Current savings
- Target amount
- Progress percentage
- Estimated completion time based on current monthly savings

---

### What-If Financial Simulator

One of the most useful features is the interactive simulator.

Users can adjust how much they reduce their monthly variable spending and instantly see:

- Increased monthly savings
- Estimated yearly savings
- Faster goal completion timelines

This demonstrates the long-term impact of small financial decisions.

---

### Financial Insights

The application automatically analyzes financial habits and generates recommendations, including:

- High fixed expense warnings
- Overspending alerts
- Subscription reviews
- Monthly savings opportunities
- Personalized budgeting advice

---

## What I Learned

### 1. Fixed expenses have the greatest impact

Large recurring costs significantly reduce monthly flexibility. Keeping fixed obligations under control is one of the most important aspects of financial planning.

### 2. Small savings create long-term impact

Reducing unnecessary spending by even a small amount each month can accumulate into substantial yearly savings.

### 3. Budget tracking prevents overspending

Comparing planned budgets with actual expenses helps identify unnecessary spending before it becomes a habit.

### 4. Goal-based saving increases motivation

Assigning savings toward specific goals makes financial planning more focused and encourages consistent progress.

### 5. Financial data is easier to understand visually

Charts, health scores, progress indicators, and interactive simulations make budgeting much more intuitive than viewing numbers alone.
