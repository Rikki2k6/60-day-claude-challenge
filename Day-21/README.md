# Day 21 - Digital Privacy Dashboard

## Objective

The objective of Day 21 was to build an interactive **Digital Privacy Dashboard** using Claude. The dashboard analyzes a user's digital footprint, privacy exposure, company data access, risk profile, and provides personalized recommendations to improve online privacy through an interactive HTML interface.

---

## Task Overview

During this challenge, I:

- Generated the complete HTML Digital Privacy Dashboard using Claude.
- Reviewed the sample digital footprint dataset.
- Analyzed my Digital Footprint Score and Privacy Score.
- Explored the Exposure Heatmap and Company Exposure Ranking.
- Reviewed the Digital Twin Profile and Risk Radar.
- Examined the Most Valuable Data Assets section.
- Explored the Privacy Improvement Simulator and personalized recommendations.
- Saved the generated HTML dashboard.
- Tested the dashboard in the browser and captured screenshots.

## Key Findings

### Exposure Heatmap

The dashboard categorized digital exposure into multiple data types:

- 🔴 Behavioural Data – Critical
- 🔴 Identity Information – Critical
- 🟠 Content Activity – High
- 🟠 Social Graph – High
- 🟡 Financial Data – Medium
- 🟡 Location Data – Medium
- 🟢 Device Information – Low-Medium
- 🟢 Gaming Data – Low-Medium

---

### Company Exposure Ranking

Highest estimated data exposure:

1. Meta
2. Google
3. ByteDance
4. Snap Inc.
5. Amazon
6. Spotify
7. Krafton

---

### Privacy Improvement Plan

The dashboard recommended several actions:

- Reduce or eliminate TikTok usage.
- Pause Google Web & App Activity and Location History.
- Disable unnecessary location permissions.
- Use Signal for sensitive conversations.
- Switch to privacy-focused search engines.
- Use separate email accounts for shopping services.
- Verify Google Photos face grouping settings.

---

## Key Learnings

- Learned how digital footprints are created across multiple online services.
- Understood how different companies collect and combine user data.
- Explored privacy scoring based on application usage and permissions.
- Learned practical methods to improve online privacy without deleting every service.
- Observed how AI can generate rich interactive dashboards using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
