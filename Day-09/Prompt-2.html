📋 Health Context
ℹ️
About Risk Analysis
Risk flags are generated based on your logged nutrient intake compared to evidence-based reference daily intakes. High-risk flags indicate intake below 50% or above 200% of RDA — these are educational signals, not diagnoses.
⚕️
When to See a Professional
Persistent deficiencies in iron, calcium, B12 or vitamin D warrant blood tests and professional evaluation. Supplement recommendations in this app are general and not prescriptive.
Nutrition Sources & Methodology
Transparency on where our data comes from
📚 Data Sources
ICMR-NIN Nutritive Value of Indian Foods (2017) — Primary source for macronutrient and micronutrient values of Indian foods including cereals, pulses, vegetables and fruits.
USDA FoodData Central (2023) — Reference values for foods not covered by ICMR-NIN, including vitamin D and B12 data.
ICMR Recommended Dietary Allowances 2020 — RDA values for protein, energy, and all micronutrients for Indian adults.
WHO/FAO Nutrient Requirements (2004) — International reference for calcium, iron, zinc and vitamin requirements.
Mifflin-St Jeor Equation (1990) — Used for Basal Metabolic Rate calculation; validated in multiple population studies as the most accurate predictive equation.
Institute of Medicine (IOM) DRI Tables — Reference values for vitamins A, C, D, E, K and B-complex vitamins.
🔬 Methodology

Energy Calculation: TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier. BMR uses Mifflin-St Jeor. Activity multipliers: Sedentary 1.2, Light 1.375, Moderate 1.55, Active 1.725, Extra 1.9.

Protein Target: Set at 1.6g per kg body weight, consistent with ICMR recommendations for moderately active adults and supported by sports nutrition research.

Macro Split: Fat at 25% of TDEE; carbohydrates fill the remainder after protein and fat calories are accounted for.

Per-100g basis: All food nutrition values are stored per 100g or 100ml. Serving size multipliers are applied based on unit selection.

Nutrient Score: A composite score (0–100) computed as the average percentage of RDA met across all tracked nutrients, capped at 100% per nutrient to avoid excess masking deficiencies.

🍱 Food Database Coverage
NutriScope v2 includes 60 foods across 8 categories. Nutrient values are per 100g unless otherwise noted.
⚠️ Limitations

• Nutrient values are averages; actual values vary by variety, region, ripeness and cooking method.

• Cooking losses (especially for vitamins C and B-complex) are not modelled in this version.

• Bioavailability differences (e.g. non-haem vs haem iron) are not accounted for in absorption estimates.

• Omega-3, omega-6, and individual amino acid profiles are not tracked.

• Sodium, potassium, magnesium, and phosphorus are not tracked in this version.

• RDA values apply to healthy adults; pregnant, lactating, elderly or ill individuals have different requirements.
