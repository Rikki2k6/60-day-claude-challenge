# Day 37

# Task Compass – Café Edition

Today I built an interactive **Task Compass** simulation using Claude. The application teaches organizational thinking by placing the user inside a café workplace where different operational scenarios must be handled by assigning responsibilities, routing tasks, and coordinating between departments.

Instead of simply answering questions, the simulator encourages users to think about **ownership, delegation, workflow, and collaboration**, showing how work actually moves through an organization.

The experience concludes with a personalized **Organizational Thinking Dashboard** that summarizes decision-making patterns and provides practical reflections.

---

## Interactive Experience

The application is divided into three learning stages:

### Stage 1 – Who Owns This?
- Analyze real workplace situations.
- Identify the primary owner responsible for resolving each task.
- Learn which supporting roles should also be involved.

### Stage 2 – Task Routing
- Arrange the correct workflow for operational scenarios.
- Build the task flow from the customer to the final resolution.
- Understand how responsibilities move between departments.

### Stage 3 – Collaboration Challenge
- Handle larger organizational problems requiring multiple teams.
- Select the departments that should collaborate.
- Compare your decisions with the recommended communication flow.

### Organizational Thinking Dashboard
After completing all stages, the simulator generates:

- Ownership score
- Delegation score
- Collaboration score
- Workflow Thinking score
- Personalized organizational reflection
- Practical workplace insights
- Option to replay the simulation

### Reflection Highlights

- Ownership still needs improvement in identifying the true first responder.
- I often focused on the primary owner without considering supporting team members.
- Strong understanding of cross-team collaboration.
- Good workflow thinking by predicting how tasks naturally move across departments.

---

## What I Learned

### 1. Every task has a clear owner

Although many people may contribute, successful organizations always define one person who owns the outcome.

### 2. Delegation is equally important

Ownership doesn't mean working alone. Identifying supporting roles helps distribute responsibilities effectively.

### 3. Collaboration solves complex problems

Operational challenges such as customer complaints, supply shortages, and viral marketing events require coordination across multiple departments.

### 4. Workflow matters

Understanding the sequence in which tasks move through an organization improves efficiency and reduces confusion.

### 5. AI can simulate workplace decision-making

Large Language Models can generate realistic organizational simulations that make learning management concepts interactive and engaging.
